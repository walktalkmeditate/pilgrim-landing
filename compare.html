Pilgrim isn't trying to replace these apps. Strava is great for athletes. AllTrails is great for hikers. Headspace is great for guided meditation. Pilgrim is for people whose walk is a practice, not a workout.

A different philosophy

Most walking apps are built to optimize your walk — faster pace, longer distance, more calories. Pilgrim is built for people who walk to think, process, and return to themselves.

Most meditation apps are built for sitting. Pilgrim is built for the path.

Most apps need your email, your data, and your subscription. Pilgrim needs nothing. No account. No cloud. No payment. Your walks stay on your phone, and when you delete the app, they go with it.

What Pilgrim does that nobody else does

Voice recording with on-device transcription. Record your thoughts while walking. WhisperKit transcribes them on your phone — nothing is uploaded, nothing leaves your device. You hear things differently when you speak them on a path.

Digital goshuin seals. In the Japanese pilgrimage tradition, temples stamp a vermilion seal in your journal at each visit. Pilgrim generates a unique digital seal for each walk, derived from the walk's distance, duration, weather, and elevation. Your collection grows with your practice.

Walking meditation with voice guides. Seven AI-generated voice guides, each with their own personality and philosophy. Breeze asks gentle questions. Stone speaks rarely and means every word. Dusk speaks in images.

Free. Open source. No accounts. No cloud.
